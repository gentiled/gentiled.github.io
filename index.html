Multi-study experimental program testing whether different types of AI explanations (counterfactual, normative, contrastive) actually help human operators make better decisions in complex process control. 150+ participants, controlled experiments, synthesised into a design framework.

Operator Performance in AI-Augmented Nuclear Control Rooms

Designed a research program studying how automation and AI transparency affect human operator performance in small modular reactor control rooms. $360K budget, 6 researchers supervised. Built the experimental paradigm from scratch using a microworld simulator. Collaboration with INL and CNSC.

AI isn’t just my tool.
It’s my subject.

Most people working on AI governance are writing policy. Most people working on AI safety are running benchmarks. I sit between those worlds: I design rigorous empirical methods to test whether AI systems do what we claim they do, for the humans who actually use them.
