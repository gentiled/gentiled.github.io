Nuclear Control Room Human Factors Verification

Human Factors Engineering Analyst · Kinectrics
task analysis · cognitive workload · hmi review · alarm management · nureg-0700 · csa n290.12

Context

Nuclear power plant operators manage thousands of alarms, indicators, and controls from a main control room. Poor interface design in this environment directly threatens plant and public safety. Regulatory frameworks require that a structured Human Factors Engineering program demonstrate the control room supports reliable human performance across normal, abnormal, and emergency plant states.

The HFE program spans the full design lifecycle—from function allocation and task analysis through verification and validation of the as-built control room.

My Role

As a Human Factors Engineering Analyst, I contributed to the verification and validation of control room designs for a new-build nuclear reactor program. My work focused on ensuring operator workstations, displays, and procedures met human factors standards and supported safe, effective operations.

What I Did

  • Conducted cognitive task analyses to map operator decision-making workflows across normal, abnormal, and emergency plant states
  • Performed workload assessments to identify high-demand scenarios and potential bottlenecks in operator capacity
  • Evaluated alarm system design—prioritization, grouping, and presentation—against regulatory guidelines
  • Reviewed human-machine interface layouts and display hierarchies using heuristic evaluation methods
  • Participated in multidisciplinary design review sessions with systems, I&C, and operations engineering teams
  • Contributed human reliability analysis inputs to probabilistic safety assessments
Heuristic evaluations were structured around NUREG-0700 guidance, covering display layout, navigation, labeling, color use, and alarm presentation.
