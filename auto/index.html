Analysed naturalistic driving data from a South Korean car rental fleet using machine learning methods. Built predictive models of collision risk from fleet telematics, producing a risk assessment framework for usage-based insurance. Published in Transportation Research Record.

HMI Evaluation Programme for Next-Generation Nuclear Control Rooms

Designed and led a $360K research programme studying how automation and AI transparency affect operator performance in small modular reactor control rooms. Built experimental paradigm from scratch using a microworld simulator. Six-person team, concept through verification.

PhD Dissertation · University of Toronto

Controlled Experiments on AI Explanations & Human Decision-Making

Five controlled experiments testing whether different types of AI explanations actually help human operators make better decisions in complex process control. Within-subjects designs, synthesised into a practitioner design framework for reliable HMI.

HMI + Automotive

The gap between what an interface promises and what a driver actually does is measurable.

As vehicles become more automated, the critical question shifts from “does the system work?” to “does the driver understand, trust, and respond correctly to the system?” — and that is a human factors problem.

I bring experimental rigour from safety-critical domains to the automotive HMI challenge: measuring how interface design, automation transparency, and information presentation affect real human performance under realistic conditions.

  • 01 Driving simulator research — experiments on vehicle automation and driver interaction within the NSERC TRAVERSAL programme (Toyota collaboration)
  • 02 Naturalistic driving data — ML risk modelling of fleet telematics for a South Korean automotive operator
  • 03 Safety-critical HMI — built and led a $360K evaluation programme for next-generation control room interfaces
  • 04 Enterprise UX research — user requirements gathering and competitive benchmarking for Ericsson’s global platform
